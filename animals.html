Explore Our Animals

Learn more about our wildlife through detailed profiles and conservation insights.

Siberian Tiger

The Siberian tiger, also known as the Amur tiger, is a subspecies native to the Russian Far East.

Asian Elephant

The Asian elephant is smaller than its African cousin and found primarily in the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

Orangutan

The orangutan is a large ape found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra, known for their intelligence and orange-red fur.

Komodo Dragon

The Komodo dragon is the largest living lizard, native to the islands of Indonesia, known for its powerful bite and venom.

Red Panda

The red panda, also known as the fire fox, is a small mammal found in the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China.

Wolverine

The wolverine is a fierce carnivore found in the northern latitudes of North America, Europe, and Asia.

Okapi

The okapi, native to the rainforests of the Democratic Republic of Congo, is a close relative of the giraffe but has a zebra-like appearance.

Harpy Eagle

The harpy eagle is a large, powerful bird of prey found in the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

Peregrine Falcon

Known as the fastest bird, the peregrine falcon can reach speeds over 240 mph while diving to catch prey.

California Condor

The California condor is one of the largest flying birds in North America, known for its massive wingspan and conservation efforts.

Great Horned Owl

The great horned owl is a large and powerful nocturnal bird of prey, known for its distinctive ear tufts and hooting call.

Scarlet Macaw

The scarlet macaw is a vibrant and intelligent parrot species, native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

Quetzal

The quetzal is a brilliantly colored bird, native to Central America, revered for its stunning feathers and cultural significance.

Andean Condor

The Andean condor is a large vulture native to South America, known for its impressive wingspan and soaring abilities.

Kea

The kea is a large, intelligent parrot native to New Zealand, known for its curiosity and playful nature, as well as its ability to solve complex problems.

Manatee

Manatees, also known as sea cows, are large, slow-moving herbivores that live in warm coastal waters and freshwater rivers.

Nile Crocodile

The Nile crocodile is a large reptile native to Africa, known for its powerful jaws and as one of the most dangerous predators in the region.

Manta Ray

Manta rays are large, graceful oceanic creatures known for their wide, triangular pectoral fins and their ability to glide effortlessly through the water.

Orca

Orcas, also known as killer whales, are highly social and intelligent apex predators, known for their coordinated hunting techniques.

Seahorse

Seahorses are small marine fish known for their unique upright posture and the male's role in pregnancy.

Axolotl

The axolotl, or Mexican walking fish, is an amphibian known for its regenerative abilities and its ability to retain larval features throughout life.

Green Sea Turtle

The Green Sea Turtle is a large, herbivorous turtle found in tropical and subtropical oceans. Known for its greenish-colored fat, it is a vulnerable species.

Clownfish

Clownfish are small, brightly colored fish known for their symbiotic relationship with sea anemones.
